# Case Study: Data Subject Rights (DSR) Walkthrough & CDP Privacy Evaluation
**Industry:** CPG – Food & Confectionery  
**Engagement Type:** Pre‑implementation Evaluation / Advisory  
**Role:** Senior Analyst / Privacy & CDP Evaluation Contributor  
**Visibility:** Portfolio‑only (not included on resume)

---

## 1. Context & Background

A global CPG client in the food and confectionery segment was evaluating the adoption of a **Customer Data Platform (CDP)** to enable unified customer views, personalized marketing, and analytics use cases.

Given the nature of consumer data involved (PII, consent, preferences, behavioral data), the client wanted to ensure that any future CDP implementation would be **privacy‑by‑design** and compliant with **GDPR and related data protection regulations**, particularly around **Data Subject Rights (DSR)**.

The engagement focused on **evaluation and readiness**, not implementation.

---

## 2. Problem Statement

While assessing CDP platforms, the client faced key challenges:

- Limited clarity on **how Data Subject Rights (DSR)** would be operationalized post‑CDP implementation  
- Lack of a clear understanding of **GDPR privacy obligations** across customer data lifecycle  
- Need for a **business‑friendly walkthrough** to explain DSR concepts to non‑legal stakeholders  
- Risk of selecting a CDP without sufficient native or extensible **privacy and consent capabilities**

---

## 3. Objectives of the Engagement

- Build a **clear, simplified walkthrough** of GDPR Data Subject Rights for business stakeholders  
- Demonstrate how DSR concepts intersect with **CDP capabilities and workflows**  
- Support the client’s **CDP evaluation process** with privacy‑focused considerations  
- Provide **recommendations** to guide CDP selection from a data protection standpoint

---

## 4. My Role & Contributions

I primarily contributed as a **privacy and CDP evaluation analyst**, focusing on research, synthesis, and client‑facing communication:

- Studied **GDPR Data Subject Rights (DSR)** and related privacy obligations  
- Interpreted regulatory requirements in the context of **customer data platforms**  
- Designed and authored **client presentations** explaining:
  - Key DSR concepts (access, erasure, rectification, portability, restriction, objection)
  - Practical implications for customer data stored in a CDP
- Created a **DSR walkthrough narrative** tailored for business, marketing, and IT stakeholders  
- Mapped **privacy requirements** to high‑level CDP capability expectations  
- Contributed **recommendations** to support privacy‑aware CDP evaluation

---

## 5. Data Subject Rights (DSR) Walkthrough – High‑Level Structure

The walkthrough was structured to be **non‑legal, intuitive, and decision‑oriented**:

- What are Data Subject Rights and why they matter to CPG organizations  
- Typical customer touchpoints where DSR requests may originate  
- How customer data flows across systems before and after CDP adoption  
- Conceptual handling of:
  - Right to Access  
  - Right to Erasure (Right to be Forgotten)  
  - Right to Rectification  
  - Right to Data Portability  
  - Consent withdrawal and preference management
- Operational considerations when a CDP acts as a **system of insight vs system of record**

---

## 6. CDP Evaluation – Privacy Lens

From a CDP evaluation standpoint, the analysis highlighted:

- Importance of **consent management and preference harmonization**
- Ability to **identify, locate, and act on customer data** across sources
- Support for **DSR workflows** (native or via integration)
- Traceability and auditability of customer data usage
- Alignment with broader **privacy governance and operating models**

---

## 7. Key Recommendations Provided

- Treat privacy and DSR readiness as a **core CDP selection criterion**, not an afterthought  
- Ensure CDP design supports **downstream DSR execution**, even if not the system of record  
- Establish **clear ownership and processes** for DSR handling across systems  
- Complement CDP capabilities with **privacy tooling and governance frameworks** where needed  
- Educate business teams early to reduce compliance risk post‑implementation

---
